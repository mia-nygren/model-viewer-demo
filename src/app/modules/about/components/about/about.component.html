ABOUT

This demo makes use of the <model-viewer> web component from Google. Angular, TypeScript, and Webpack was used when developing and building this demo.

Interactivity

The <model-viewer> component is used to view and interact with the models. You can rotate a 3D-model by dragging with your mouse (desktop) or finger (mobile).

3D-models and images

The 3D-models were created in blender 2.8 release candidate and the still images were rendered in blender cycles render engine. The models were UV-unwrapped and textured, and after packing the UVs the materials were baked to three separate images: Diffuse, Normal Map, and Metallic/Roughness combined. Metallic is the blue channel and roughness the green channel.

Diffuse Normal Map Metallic/Roughness

Some post processing was made in Photoshop, as well as image optimization.

Blender glTF 2.0 exporter was used to export the 3D models to the glTF format.

This has been my first attempt to export and create 3D-models for the web, hence I discovered that more optimization is needed. They sofa model and the ukulele model needs to be reduced further in file-size by making them more low poly. Unfortunately model-viewer does not support draco-compression at the moment, which could have reduced the file size of the glTF models by around 45%.
